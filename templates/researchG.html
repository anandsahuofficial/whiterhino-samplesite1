Synthesis and Stabilization of Multi-Electron Acceptors and Multi-Electron Donors


We have applied different types of non-covalent intramolecular interactions e.g., H-bonding (n*), P---O (n*), Halogen-bonding (n*), Carbonyl---Cyano (n*) to stabilize Radical Ions and other Redox-active electron-rich or electron-deficient molecules


We could stabilize and isolate the first air-stable NDI-based radical ions and its Ultra-electron-deficient precursor by applying intramolecular P---O (n*) interactions;

J. Am. Chem. Soc. 2014, 136, 12004.

Chem. Sci.2019, 10, 6482-6493

We utilized intramolecular N-H---O H-bonding (n*) interactions to stabilize Radical Anions as well as Radical Cations

Chem. Eur. J. 2017, 23, 11802-11809

We have undertaken a study on the halogen bonding properties of arylene-imide/-diimide-based electron deficient scaffolds. We probed the influence of: scaffold size, e.g., from the small phthalimide (PTMI), moderately-sized pyromelliticdiimide (PMDI) or naphthalenediimides (NDIs) to the large perylenediimide (PDI); axial-group modifications; varied number of halogens, etc. on the halogen bonding and its self-assembly in a set of nine molecules.We realized single crystals of all the nine systems, which revealed Br∙∙∙O, Br∙∙∙Br or Br∙∙∙π halogen bonding interactions, with few systems capable of forming all the three-types. These interactions lead to halogen bonded rings (up to 12-membered), which propagate to form stacked 1D-, 2D- or corrugated sheets.Computational studies based on Atoms in Molecules (AIM) and Natural Bond Orbital (NBO) analysis provided further insight into the halogen bonding interactions

Kalyanashis, et al.
Chem. Eur. J. 2020, 26, 10607-10619
