Pritam Mukhopadhyay


Dr. Pritam Mukhopadhyay

Associate Professor
School of Physical Sciences
Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi




Pritam Mukhopadhyay obtained his B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees from Calcutta University, India. He obtained his Ph.D. from IIT Kanpur, India under the guidance of Prof. ParimalKanti Bharadwaj. In his Ph.D., he got skilled in the synthesis of functionalized macrocycles, studies on molecular recognition,and their applications to nonlinear optically active systems. He then joined the group of Prof. Lyle Isaacs at the University of Maryland, College Park, USA as a post-doctoral fellow, where he worked on the synthesis and studies of complex self-sorting systems in aqueous solution. Later, he had the opportunity to work with Prof. SeijiShinkaiand Dr.Norifumi Fujita at the Kyushu University, Japan, as a JSPS fellow, and got trained in the synthesis and engineering of organic soft-materials and self-healing systems. He joined the School of Physical Sciences, Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi, India in Dec. 2006, and currently is an Associate Professor. Pritam has been an Associate of the Indian Academy of Sciences, Bangalore. He is also the recipient of SwarnaJayanti Fellowship, Govt. of India.

His group’s research interests involve various aspects of design, synthesis and stabilization of organic radical ions. One of the major achievements of the group has been to stabilize various types of organic radicals, e.g., radical anions, radical cations and zwitterionic radicals through intramolecular non-covalent interactions. The isolated organic radicals have been found to be ambient stable and, in some cases, even stable to chromatography.

The group’s other areas of interest include synthesis of organic multi-electron redox active systems. Research in this area has led to the isolation of some of the strongest electron acceptors and also electron donors, including electron-sponges and electron-reservoirs.

In addition, the group is interested in the design and synthesis of organic molecular and supramolecular materials, e.g., organic near-infrared and mid-IR absorbers, panchromatic molecules, multi-channel radical-based sensors, organic semiconductors, organic ferroelectrics, organic piezoelectrics, and organic NLO-active materials.

Along with these areas, the group has started working on the synthesis of organic redox-active drugs as well as hybrid drug molecules.

The research scholars in the group are trained in organic and organometallic synthesis, purification methods and handling of reactive catalysts and compounds. The scholars get trained in various spectroscopic, spectrometric and electrochemical techniques, as well as proficient in growing of single crystals and single crystal X-ray crystallography. The students also acquire skills in the basic and advanced aspects of computational studies.
