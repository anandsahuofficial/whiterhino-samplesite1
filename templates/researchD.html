Organic n-type Semiconductors

Supramolecular Materials IVa. Organic n-type Semiconductors: A facile access to octabromo-perylene-3,4,9,10-tetracarboxylic dianhydride (Br8-PDA),its diimides (Br8-PDIs) and functionalized diimides is reported. Notably, Br8-PDAis soluble in CHCl3/CH2Cl2, a unique feature for a PDA scaffold. Octabrominationresults in low reduction potentials [E1R/E2R (Br8-PDA) = +0.06/-0.16 V vs SCE], radical anion formation and red-shifted optical properties. For the first time, diverse halogen bonding interactions were identified in the PDA/PDI, which along with electronic features enhance the electron transport characteristics and the n-type semiconductor properties compared to the di-/tetra-brominated PDIs (Br2-PDIs/Br4-PDIs).

Yogendra and co-workers,
Org. Lett. 2016, 18, 472−475.

Confluence of a modular design approach and self-assembly with a ‘steroid-D-π-A’ module generates spontaneous polarization in single crystalline solids and for the first time in nano-architectures constituted from organogels, at room temperature (RT).

Deepak and co-workers,

Magnetic, High-Resistive Radical Ions

We recently reported the synthesis, ionic-assembly, magnetic and electronic properties of three NDI-based ambient stable radical ions with Br-,BPh4-and BF4-counter anions. The radical ions werecharacterizedbymeans of single-crystal X-ray diffractionstudiesrevealing extensive anion- and hydrogen-bonding interactions within the radical ions and with the counter anions. Thus, radicalions could be organized to form infinite supramolecular arrays. Magnetic studies revealed their paramagnetic nature in the range of 10 to 300 K as well as high resistivity approaching giga-ohm.

Sharvan and co-workers,
Chem. Eur. J. 2019, 25, 4740.

Supramolecular Tuning of NLO Property

Urea-based molecular constructs are shown for the first time to be nonlinear optically (NLO) active in solution. We demonstrate self-assembly triggered large amplification and specific anion recognition driven attenuation of the NLO activity. This orthogonal modulation along with an excellent nonlinearity-transparency trade-off makes them attractive NLO probes for studies related to weak self-assembly and anion transportation by second harmonic microscopy

Deepak and co-workers,
Chem. Commun. 2013, 49, 451-453 (Cover Article).
