Highly Electron-rich Zwitterionic Radicals and Electron Transfer Properties

Electron donor ability of organic radicals has not been attempted due to instability, and reactivity challenges. We have now been able to synthesize and isolate, Naphthalenediimide-based (NDI·±) zwitterionic radicals, which bestow SOMO levels up to -4.04 eV. As a result, reduction of electron-deficient NDI is realized with NDI·± radicals in their ground-states. Notably, reduction of the NDI unit applying a -electron donor is unprecedented, and has been limited to inorganic/organometallic reagents or by light-driven excited-states.

Jyoti, Sharvan and co-workers,
Org. Lett.2020, 22, 6229-6233. (Cover Article).

Synthesis and Stabilization of Radical Ions and Highly Electron Deficient Molecules, Applying Intramolecular P---O Interactions

Stabilization of Radical Cations and Radical Anions Applying H-bonding Interactions

We investigated the influence of H-bonding interactions towards the stabilization of NDI-based radical cations and radical anions. The core-substituted amino-NDIs could form persistent radical cations upon oxidation and radical anions upon reduction, respectively. The life-times of the radicals could be influenced depending on the intramolecular H-bonding interactions.In particular, the radical cations showed sharp absorption at 870 nm. .


M. R. Ajayakumar,et al.
Org. Lett. 2012,14, 4822.
